Abstract

Recent advancements in imitation learning have demonstrated significant potential in autonomous driving. However, existing methods often suffer from excessive intervention rates and frequent collisions in complex urban environments, indicating a considerable gap from practical application. In this paper, we introduce a novel planning framework, PLUTO, which pushes the limits of imitation learning-based planners through three key innovations. First, we propose a longitudinal-lateral aware model architecture that enables more diverse and adaptive driving behaviors. Second, we design an efficient and generalizable auxiliary loss for enhanced scene understanding and constraint satisfaction. Finally, we present a new training paradigm, Contrastive Imitation Learning (CIL), along with novel data augmentations, to regularize driving behavior and improve generalization. Our method sets a new state-of-the-art on the nuPlan benchmark, outperforming all previous learning-based methods and top-ranked rule-based planners. Notably, PLUTO achieves a 20% improvement in terms of driven miles and a 35% reduction in collisions compared to the previous state-of-the-art learning-based method.
